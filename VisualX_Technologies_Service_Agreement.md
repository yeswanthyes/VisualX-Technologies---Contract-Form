## 14. FORCE MAJEURE

### 14.1 Definition

Neither Party shall be held liable for any failure or delay in the performance of its obligations under this Agreement if such failure or delay results from events beyond the reasonable control of the affected Party ("Force Majeure Events"), including but not limited to:

- Acts of God: natural disasters, earthquakes, floods, hurricanes, epidemics, pandemics
- Government actions: imposition of sanctions, embargoes, regulatory changes, lockdowns
- Infrastructure failures: power outages, internet outages, server failures, data center outages
- Third-party service disruptions: payment gateway outages, cloud service provider failures, API discontinuation
- Civil unrest: war, terrorism, riots, strikes, labor disputes
- Cyberattacks: DDoS attacks, ransomware, data breaches not attributable to negligence

### 14.2 Notification

The affected Party shall notify the other Party in writing within **forty-eight (48) hours** of the occurrence of a Force Majeure Event, providing:

- A description of the event
- The expected impact on the project
- An estimated duration of the delay
- Proposed mitigation measures

### 14.3 Mitigation

The affected Party shall take all reasonable steps to mitigate the impact of the Force Majeure Event and resume performance of its obligations as soon as practicable.

### 14.4 Prolonged Force Majeure

If a Force Majeure Event continues for a period exceeding **sixty (60) days**, either Party may terminate this Agreement by providing **fifteen (15) days' written notice**. In such event, the Client shall pay for all work completed up to the date of termination, and the Company shall deliver all completed work products.
